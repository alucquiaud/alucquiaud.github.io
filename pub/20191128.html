Urban segregation: a micro-mobility induced phenomenon displaying multiscalar complexity

A. Lucquiaud


Human mobility: from big data to applications, La Havana, Cuba (28/11/2019)

Abstract
We present a multifocal approach to reveal spatial dissimilarities in real cities, from the most local scale to the metropolitan one. Any statistical variable (e.g. social housing rate, ethnic group proportions) can be measured at larger and larger scales, starting from any unit block, and yields a beam of sequences revealing the city's underlying spatial structure.

At the individual scale, Schelling's model of segregation is useful to understand the emergence of this structure. Although it has been actively studied in various fields from economics to statistical physics, its spatial nature has not been adressed analytically yet. We will present an analytical derivation using Markov chains to determine the dynamics and thermodynamics of such a system.
